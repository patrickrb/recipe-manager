Shrimp Scampi Pasta

Italian, Seafood, Shrimp

Ingredients

8 oz pasta (linguine or spaghetti work great)


1 lb frozen shrimp (thawed, peeled, deveined, tails on or off)


3–4 tablespoons unsalted butter


2 tablespoons olive oil


4 cloves garlic, minced


1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)


1/2 cup dry white wine (or chicken broth)


Juice and zest of 1 lemon


Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste


2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley


Grated parmesan (optional)

Directions

1. Prep the Shrimp:
Thaw shrimp under cold water if frozen. Pat dry with paper towels.


2. Cook Pasta:
Boil pasta in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain.


3. Sauté Shrimp:

Heat 2 tablespoons butter and 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

Add shrimp in a single layer. Season lightly with salt and pepper.

Cook about 1-2 minutes per side, until just pink and opaque. Remove shrimp and set aside.



4. Make Scampi Sauce:

In the same skillet (add a touch more oil if needed), lower heat to medium.

Add garlic and red pepper flakes; sauté until fragrant (30 seconds—don’t let garlic brown).

Pour in white wine (or broth). Scrape up any bits from the pan. Simmer for 2 minutes to reduce slightly.

Add lemon juice and zest.



5. Finish:

Return shrimp (and any juices) to the pan. Add cooked pasta.

Toss everything together, adding reserved pasta water a little at a time until it’s saucy.

Taste and adjust seasoning (salt, pepper, more lemon if you like).

Stir in remaining tablespoon of butter until glossy.



6. Serve:

Sprinkle with fresh parsley and parmesan (if using).

Serve with lemon wedges and maybe some crusty bread to mop up the sauce.

Notes

Tips

If you want it richer, add a splash of cream at the end.

You can add halved cherry tomatoes or baby spinach for extra color/veggies.

If you’re not into wine, chicken broth works just fine.
